About Mary Jatkowski

When I completed my first digital project as an undergraduate student at the University of Michigan, I knew this was work I wanted to do for the rest of my career. The project involved digitizing a 1930s Hopi Native American collection in an attempt to repatriate the artifacts to its source community; provide the infrastructure for the Hopi community to interpret the collection; and ultimately, make the collection more accessible to the Hopi farmers and community, and the University students and faculty. It was eye-opening to see how the irreplicable collection could be digitally shared with people, no matter how remote. Through this project, I realized that the best way to serve museums and education is to develop digital platforms that allow users to access cultural materials all over the world while providing the means for long-term preservation.

I am now pursuing a graduate degree in Library and Information science to advance my expertise in website design, information sharing, and digital preservation to equip me for assisting museums in digitization. I now live in beautiful Charleston, SC where I continue to design websites, mobile apps, and digital content for museums and historic non-profits. I hope to continue to serve these precious storehouses of information and community centers by offering my services in creating accessible, engaging online platforms to increase the impact and reach of each museum and organization.
